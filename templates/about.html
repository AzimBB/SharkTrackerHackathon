About the Challenge

Earth's ocean is one of the most powerful habitats in our universe, supporting a range of life that sustains ecosystems and habitability across the globe.

It is common to measure photosynthetic activity in the ocean from space, but far more challenging to track top predators. Your challenge is to create a mathematical framework for identifying sharks and predicting their foraging habitats using NASA satellite data, and also to suggest a new conceptual model of a tag (a small electronic device that can be attached to an animal to track and study its movement) that could measure not only where sharks are, but also what they are eating, and in real time transmit that data back to users to enable development of predictive models. (Earth Science Division)

Our Approach

We're using NASA satellite data including MODIS for sea surface temperature and chlorophyll-a concentration, SWOT for sea surface height anomalies, and GEBCO bathymetry data to create a comprehensive habitat suitability index for various shark species.

Key Technologies Used:
  • Python with Flask for backend processing
  • JavaScript with Leaflet for interactive mapping
  • Xarray for handling multi-dimensional scientific data
  • Machine learning algorithms for pattern recognition
  • Real-time data visualization techniques
