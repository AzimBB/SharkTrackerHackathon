Frequently Asked Questions

How do sharks use oceanographic features for hunting?

Sharks, particularly white sharks, use anticyclonic eddies as hunting grounds. These rotating water masses create upwelling that brings nutrients to the surface, attracting prey species. The eddies also concentrate prey through convergent currents, making them highly efficient hunting areas.

What environmental factors influence shark habitat suitability?

Shark habitat suitability is determined by four key factors:

  • Sea Surface Temperature (SST): Sharks are temperature-sensitive species that prefer specific thermal ranges
  • Chlorophyll-a Concentration: Indicates primary productivity and potential prey availability
  • Sea Surface Height Anomaly (SSHa): Identifies oceanographic features like eddies and fronts
  • Bathymetry (Depth): Determines suitable foraging depths for different species

Why are white sharks temperature-sensitive?

White sharks are endothermic, meaning they can regulate their body temperature above the surrounding water temperature. However, they still prefer specific thermal ranges for optimal physiological function:

  • They prefer water temperatures between 12-24Â°C (optimal 15-22Â°C)
  • Colder waters require more energy to maintain body temperature
  • Warmer waters may stress their physiological systems
  • Temperature influences prey distribution and availability

How do anticyclonic eddies enhance foraging opportunities?

Anticyclonic eddies (rotating clockwise in the Northern Hemisphere) create favorable conditions for foraging:

  • They trap and concentrate prey through convergent currents
  • Upwelling at the edges brings nutrients to the surface
  • They create temperature gradients that attract different species
  • Prey becomes disoriented in the rotating currents, making it easier to catch
